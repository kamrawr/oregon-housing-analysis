Executive Summary for Program Staff

This comprehensive database synthesizes residential housing data across all 36 Oregon counties, providing essential information for program design, outreach targeting, and policy development. The data reveals significant regional disparities and highlights Oregon's deepening housing crisis.

36 Counties Analyzed

50%+ Renters Cost-Burdened

Key Program Targeting Insights

  • High-Need Counties: Portland metro (high costs), rural eastern counties (limited resources)
  • Energy Program Focus: 75% of housing units eligible for Energy Trust programs through IOU utilities
  • Housing Types: Single-family dominates rural areas; multifamily concentrated in urban centers
  • Income Disparities: $118K AMI in Portland metro vs. $80.9K minimum in 22 rural counties
  • Rental Market: Concentrated in urban areas with severe affordability challenges
  • Homeownership Gaps: Significant racial disparities requiring targeted interventions

Housing Type Classifications

Single Family Housing

  • Traditional Single Family Detached: Standalone houses with open space on all sides
  • Manufactured/Modular Housing: Factory-built homes, significant in rural areas
  • Single Family with ADUs: Houses with accessory dwelling units (policy changes since 2019)

Small Multifamily Housing (2-4 units)

  • Duplexes: Two-unit buildings, common in urban infill
  • Triplexes/Quadplexes: 3-4 unit buildings in neighborhood commercial areas
  • Cottage Clusters: Small-scale clustered housing developments
  • Infill Housing: Small-scale housing on previously developed lots

Large Multifamily Housing

  • Small Apartments (5-19 units): Medium-density housing in suburban areas
  • Medium Apartments (20-99 units): Higher-density complexes in urban centers
  • Large Apartments (100+ units): High-density housing in metropolitan areas

Utility Service Territory Analysis

74% IOU Utility Customers
26% Consumer-Owned Utility

Investor-Owned Utilities (74% of Oregon Customers)

Energy Trust Eligible: Customers of investor-owned utilities are eligible for Energy Trust of Oregon programs, providing access to energy efficiency rebates and renewable energy incentives.

Electric Service

  • Portland General Electric (PGE): 780,000 residential customers in Portland metro area
  • Pacific Power: 517,000 residential customers across eastern, southern, and parts of central Oregon
  • Idaho Power: 13,500 residential customers in Malheur County area
  • Avista: Small service area in eastern Oregon (Union/Wallowa counties)

Natural Gas Service

  • NW Natural: 607,000 residential customers (80% of state gas delivery), primarily urban western Oregon
  • Cascade Natural Gas: ~78,500 customers in central and eastern Oregon
  • Avista Natural Gas: ~78,500 customers in southern and eastern Oregon

Consumer-Owned Utilities (26% of Oregon Customers)

37 consumer-owned electric utilities serve remaining customers, primarily in coastal and rural areas with limited Energy Trust access.

Energy Trust Coverage Analysis

Approximately 75-80% of Oregon housing units are eligible for Energy Trust programs through their utility service. Counties with full Energy Trust access show higher rates of energy efficiency improvements and renewable energy adoption.
